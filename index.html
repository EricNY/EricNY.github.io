Week 7

Nov 1st, 2013

So I’m in the elevator yesterday right? And one of my classmates who is also in there you know… he turns and says to me he says… he says to me… get this… he says to me… your’e not gonna believe it… he says to me “I can’t tell if our time here at Fullstack is flying by or if it feels like we’ve been here forever.” Can you believe it? Can you believe he said that?!

Dont ask me why I felt the need to talk that way just now. It’s early. Anyway, I feel the same way as my classmate. Even our teacher David looked at the log of assignments we’ve all done and commenting on the number of projects in there he said “Wow, we’ve been through so much together!”. It seems to be a pervasive feeling around the classroom that while we have all learned a ton of stuff at breakneck speed the end is almost here. I guess 7 weeks of full time learning can be considered a long time for a class we all still feel like there is so much more to learn.

This week we did some JQuery work and were introduced to Backbone. Now the bulk of the learning new technologies and languages is basically over and it’s project time. I need to finalize an idea for a capstone project. I have some ideas but want to run them by Nimit and David first.

But this is probably just what I need in regards to my feeling about how my time here is spent. I have been introduced to a lot of new things and done some cool stuff but I need to crystallize my knowledge of the material. No better way to do that than to work through projects and gain experience using the tools.

Halfway Point

Oct 25th, 2013

Halfway point is here, this is the end of week 6. We started with frontend development this week which meant brushing up on our HTML and CSS and getting into Javascript.

I wrote a lot of code using JS in preparing for this course but it certainly took some time to get used to it again since we’ve been focused on using Ruby so much these past few weeks. The principles are still the same no matter what language you use like recursion, iteration, various data structures, etc… they just each have their own way of implementing them.

I am getting more comfortable using various tools in my “box”. Apparently that is the cool programmer way to refer to my computer! Lol. I have a lot more to learn though regarding my development environment, even my text editor has more tricks up its sleeve that I still need to get familiar with.

This week we started Fullstack’s “CTO Program” talks. We had a group discussion with Dave where we talked about concepts from Eric Reis’ book The Lean Startup. It is a very exciting time to be a web developer since we can start a business with basically nothing. Of course scaling is an whole other matter but it has been hammered home to us that we should not focus on scaling issues until the time comes where it is necessary. Until then dont let it get in the way of trying out new ideas.

End Week 5

Oct 18th, 2013

This week we had our longest project yet. We built a crowdsourcing app similar to Kickstarter. It was very involved and at times things got frustrating but I worked with two great partners in Kyle and Julian. I feel there are times when it makes sense to work alone but it absolutely makes sense to work with others at other times, especially for large projects! We worked on breaking the whole app down into smaller tasks and assigning them out. It’s important to get along with and respect your teammates and these guys were great! There were some times I felt I was struggling to keep up but it kept me motivated to learn more and I hope they enjoyed working with me as well.

Our app allows users to log in and create a profile. Then they can either start their own fundraising campaign and/or donate to someone elses campaign. I think we will win the prize for sweetest layout! I was able to put a status bar on the top of the campaign page that fills up as people donate and I put in a picture carousel that scrolls through pictures that a campaign creator uploads. Julian is working on making the entire background play a video behind all the content, looked really good when he was testing it – lets see if we can incorporate it in time!

I wanted to add a comment section but ran out of time. But then again, maybe I shouldn’t per our class discussion on app development best practices. Nimit and David recommended we read a book called Getting Real by 37 Signals which is the company that developed Rails. In it there is an reference to the “less is more” philosophy and we should not try to put too much in an app just because we can. Focusing on the “must haves” and omitting the “don’t needs” will lead to a more elegant design. Although, while we are just learning, I admit it’s fun to add in cool things like a video background, comment section, a robot monkey that can do your taxes, etc…

Overall I am starting to see the power of the Rails framework and I like how we took the time for an “under the hood” approach to understanding it. I hope to go through Michael Hartl’s tutorial on it again soon and reinforce what I’ve been doing.

-E

P.S. We have not pushed our crowdsourcing app to the internet yet but will post a link when we do. I was pushing to name it FullTilt (a cross between Fullstack Academy and CrowdTilt) but we settled on Crowdcontrol.

Week 3

Oct 4th, 2013

OK. Week 3 has come and gone and things are getting intense. We started taking the different pieces we have been learning and putting them together. The projects are getting more difficult but I am starting to see the bigger picture of what is involved with web development and its very exciting.

This week we used ActiveRecord and SQL to access more IMDB data. We had to write code for an empty model.

We also used Sinatra to build our first fully functioning web app. We call it Stackworld where a user can add a personal profile and create their own world where different rooms can link to eachother and they can navigate through the world.

Lastly, we brushed up on regular expressions and we just got our new exciting which I feel will be difficult but its a really cool thing to know. We will be building a web crawler which will allow us to scrape websites for data and create a graph using that data. Apparently this is some serious technology so we all made a pact to use our new powers for good instead of evil:)

Week 2 Update

Hello all,

We are finishing up week 2 here at Fullstack Academy and I couldnt be happier! This week we wrote an AI program that plays Blackjack, we worked on getting a basic working knowledge of SQL, and we learned how to use Ruby code to connect to and query an SQLite database of IMDB data.

End of Week 1 a Retrospective

Sep 20th, 2013

We are wrapping up week one here at Fullstack and I think we covered a good amount of work but I feel like it will only get more intense from here. Something tells me there will be many evenings where I dont get home until verrry late:)

The most interesting thing we did this week was probably our mailer assignment from yesterday. We got to use CSV files, .erb files, Ruby and HTML and we had to make them all work together. The final result was to be able to send an email or text to a list of friends notifying them of our latest blog entry.

Something I struggle with is understanding what is going on with the code we are writing, specifically for this mailer assignment. While it is very interesting stuff and the code I wrote works I feel I do not have a deep understanding of what we just did. I want to review this project when I get the chance.

I like the format of the class. David and Nimit give us enough lecture time so we cover various concepts but we also have plenty of time to actually get down to business and work through coding challenges ourselves.

Pair programming is new to me but I can see the benefits. Sometimes it was unclear which one of us was in the driver’s seat as we talked through problems but it definitely helps to have a sounding board for your ideas as we are working through a challenge together.

This weekend I plan on reviewing what we did in class this week as well as some of the pre-work.

-Eric
